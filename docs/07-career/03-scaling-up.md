# Scaling Up

The skills that win your first $500 contract are not the ones that build a $3,000+/month practice. Phase one was about *proving* value; scaling is about *pricing, packaging, and positioning* it. Here are the moves, roughly in the order they become available.

## Move 1 — The retainer floor

Income volatility, not low rates, is what actually breaks freelancers. The fix is the retainer floor: **3–5 recurring clients covering your baseline costs** before you optimize anything else.

The math that makes it work: a client paying $400/month for a defined block (regression each release + exploratory on new features + same-day fix verification — the [conversion script](../05-freelancing/05-client-management.md#from-gig-to-retainer)) costs you ~12–15 predictable hours. Four such clients = a planable $1,600/month floor at perhaps 55 hours — *before* any new business. Every hour saved by [automating](../03-automation/01-when-to-automate.md) their regression widens your margin at constant price: automation isn't just a skill upgrade, it's a **margin engine** unique to QA retainers.

Retainer hygiene: written scope per block, unused hours don't roll, overage at full rate, quarterly value review (show the numbers: bugs caught, releases protected — make renewal a formality).

## Move 2 — The specialization bet

Generalists compete with everyone; specialists compete with dozens. Pick **one** axis and re-aim your profile, portfolio, and content at it:

| Axis | Example position | Why it scales |
|---|---|---|
| **Vertical** | "QA for Shopify/e-commerce stores" | Domain knowledge compounds; referrals cluster within industries; you reuse the same [regression skeleton](../02-core-craft/01-writing-test-cases.md#organizing-suites) endlessly |
| **Technical** | "Playwright automation + CI for startups" | Rides the [bifurcation](../04-remote-market/01-market-overview.md#the-bifurcation--and-your-strategy); rates 2–3x manual |
| **Niche skill** | "Accessibility audits (WCAG/EAA)" | Legal tailwind since mid-2025, [thin supply](../01-foundations/03-testing-types-and-levels.md#non-functional-types-how-well-it-works) |
| **Stage** | "Pre-launch QA for funded MVPs" | Clear buyer (founders), clear moment, premium urgency pricing |

The test of a real specialization: your headline names a *client*, not a tool list — and prospects say "that's exactly us." You can always take work outside the niche; you market inside it.

## Move 3 — Productized services

Custom-scoped work has a sales cost on every deal. Packaging kills it: fixed scope, fixed price, named deliverable — the [starter audit](../05-freelancing/02-profile-optimization.md#the-starter-audit--your-conversion-machine) was the first rung; the scaled menu looks like:

- **Pre-Launch QA Package** ($500–1,500): plan + full functional pass + device matrix + regression checklist + summary. For the "we ship in 3 weeks" founder.
- **Automation Starter** ($1,000–3,000): 10–15 critical-path Playwright tests + CI wiring + handover docs + a month of maintenance. Naturally upsells into a retainer.
- **Release Guardian retainer** ($400–1,200/mo): the Move-1 block, productized with a name — names sell.
- **Accessibility Audit** ($400–900): WCAG scan + manual keyboard/screen-reader pass + prioritized remediation report.

Productization also fixes your *pricing* psychology: clients compare a package against the problem ("is launch confidence worth $900?"), not against another freelancer's hourly rate.

## Move 4 — Up-market and off-platform

- **Climb the platform ladder**: with 12–18 months of reviews, apply to the [elite networks](../04-remote-market/02-platforms-and-marketplaces.md#tier-4--elite-networks) (Toptal, Lemon.io, Arc) where blended rates start where marketplace rates peak.
- **Direct clients** (no platform fee, real relationships): sources in order of yield — referrals from existing clients (ask explicitly at every happy ending: "know anyone else shipping fast without QA?"), your LinkedIn content flywheel, niche communities where your specialization's buyers gather, and partnerships with dev agencies that build but don't test (one agency relationship can fill a calendar — pitch them a white-label QA arrangement).
- **Platform-independence discipline** ([why](../04-remote-market/02-platforms-and-marketplaces.md#choosing-your-two)): own a one-page site with your packages and case studies; keep client relationships (compliantly) portable; never let one platform exceed ~60% of income for long.

## Move 5 — Choose your shape: solo expert vs micro-agency

Around $4–6K/month of demand, your calendar fills and you must choose:

**Solo expert** — raise rates until demand balances calendar; keep margins (90%+), keep autonomy, accept the income ceiling of one person's hours. Best when your value is *judgment* (exploratory, strategy, audits). Many of the happiest careers in QA live here permanently.

**Micro-agency** — subcontract execution (a junior runs regression passes you've systematized; you own clients, strategy, and review). Margins drop (30–50% on subcontracted hours), management becomes your job, but income decouples from your hours and the *systematized assets* you've built (suite skeletons, checklists, report templates) become genuinely leverageable. Start with exactly one trusted junior on exactly one well-documented retainer; scale only what survives contact with delegation.

There's also the **hybrid exit**: scaled freelancers are extremely attractive senior/lead hires — a $130K+ remote QA lead role with your client-facing scars is a legitimate "scaling move" too ([Getting Hired](../06-remote-work/02-getting-hired.md)).

## What to keep investing in

- **Reputation assets**: case studies with numbers ("caught 2 launch-blocking payment bugs; release shipped on time") beat skills lists forever. Collect them deliberately, with permission.
- **Craft depth**: the [AI shift](../03-automation/04-ai-in-qa.md) keeps repricing execution downward and judgment upward; stay on the judgment side via community, conferences, and deliberate practice.
- **A bench of peers**: the freelancers one tier above you are your referral network for overflow work in both directions. Generosity with leads compounds.

:::tip The metric that matters now

Stop tracking income alone; track **income per worked hour, trailing 90 days**. Every move on this page — retainers, specialization, productization, up-market, delegation — is just a different lever on that one number. When a move stops moving it, switch levers.

:::
